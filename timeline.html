Built a controller for a toaster oven to maintain the different temperature state for the reflow process on surface mount PCB components. Temperature is regulated with an AT89LP52 microcontroller which will activate a solid state replay based on the desired and the input temperature from a thermocouple. The system displays reflow profile selection, state, run time, and current temperature. They system is outputing to a laptop which will plot the temperature. The system also has a servo motor that will open the door at the appropiate time to accellerate cooling. The laptop also will send an email to indicate the process is complete.

Built an alarm clock Wiring of breadboard and soldering were require to connect the AT89LP52, speaker, push buttons and LCD screen. Using interrupts in assembly, the alarm clock is able to set the time and alarm, and play a song at the corresponding alarm time. To turn off the alarm, one must press the corresponding arrow buttons to the arrows appearing on the screen, failing to do so, will cause the alarm to snooze and ring again in 5 mins.
